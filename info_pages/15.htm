AWS PROFESSIONAL CERTIFICATIONS

Certified Welding Inspector (CWI)

CWIs determine if a weldment meets acceptance criteria, handle qualification records, oversee non-destructive testing, and ensure proper materials are available during testing. The AWS Certified Welding Inspector credential is one of the welding industry's most recognized certifications. In fact, CWIs are highly regarded and sought after throughout the welding industry. 

Learn More

Senior Certified Welding Inspector (SCWI)

The SCWI credential is for experienced CWIs who have a deeper understanding of advanced NDE topics, quality systems, procedure qualifications, and other areas of expertise. The SCWI is the highest level of certification offered by AWS and recognized industry-wide.
Learn More

Certified Welding Supervisor (CWS)

The CWS Program gives supervisor candidates the ability to demonstrate their skills, training, and knowledge to be able to lead their team to work even more productively and successfully.

Learn More

Certified Welding Educator (CWE)

The CWE Program was developed for welding industry professionals who seek to demonstrate the necessary skills, knowledge, and experience to direct and perform operations associated with welder training and classroom instruction.

Learn More

Certified Welding Engineer (CWENG)

The CWENG credential is granted to a candidate who has documented training and qualifying work experience per requirements of AWS B5.16:2006 – Specification for the Qualification of Welding Engineers, and who has received a passing exam score.

Learn More

Certified Resistance Welding Technician (CRWT)

The CRWT credential is a benchmark for the evaluation of demonstrated skills and abilities in resistance welding principles, processes, and equipment through a combination of education, experience, and examination.

Learn More

Certified Radiographic Interpreter (CRI)

The CRI credential validates the candidate’s knowledge, skills, and abilities to utilize radiographic film and related media for welding indications, and use acceptance criteria as expressed in the AWS, API, and ASME codes.

Learn More

Certified Robotic Arc Welding (CRAW)

The CRAW certification program allows operators and technicians in various welding sectors to measure themselves against occupational standards and signifies their capability of working with various codes, standards, and specifications.

Learn More

Certified Welding Sales Representative (CWSR)

Welding Sales Representatives are the conduit between manufacturers and distributors and their customer base. The CWSR credential indicates an individual is a valued source of technological, welding product, and sales distribution information.

Learn More

Certified Associate Welding Inspector (CAWI)

The CAWI credential was developed for young professionals who aspire to a career in welding inspection but still need to meet the minimum work experience requirement to become a Certified Welding Inspector (CWI).

Learn More

Certified Welder Program

The Certified Welder program is a performance-based program with no prerequisite courses or certifications required. Final certification provides “transferrable” credentials that you may take with you wherever you go.

Learn More

Facility Accreditation

Facility Accreditation

A Competitive Advantage

AWS offers a number of facility accreditations to help organizations show that they operate at a higher standard. These include Accredited Test Facilities, which play an integral part in the AWS Certified Welding program; Certified Welding Fabricators, which show an organizations dedication to quality; and Certified Robotic Arc Welding Testing Center accreditations, which let organizations test the capabilities of individuals in this area.

Learn More

International Certifications

International Certifications

Since 1919, American Welding Society standards, services, and programs have supported the men and women of welding in the United States.

Today AWS is proud to say that our influence spans the globe, and we support thousands of welding professionals worldwide. To assist our international members, AWS contracts with organizations in countries throughout the globe to facilitate certification exams.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • What do I need to know about computer-based testing when it comes to taking AWS exams related to certifications?

    Computer-based testing, or CBT, is a method of proctored in-person exam delivery using a computer at an AWS Exam Center. You can learn more about what that means here. We’ve also created a tutorial to help with all things CBT. You can view it here.

  • What are “endorsements” and what do I need to know about them if I’m interested in becoming a Certified Welding Inspector (CWI) or Senior Certified Welding Inspector (SCWI)?

    Endorsements are supplemental inspection credentials available to all CWIs and SCWIs. They’re meant to enhance a welding inspector’s credentials, not supplant them. Learn more about Endorsements

  •  I have questions about a specific certification. Where can I find answers?

    The best place to look is on the page of our site that’s dedicated to the particular certification you’re considering. Each of those pages has a collection of specific FAQs to help answer your questions. If you still can’t find the answer, feel free to contact us—we’re happy to help.

  • I’m a military veteran. Are there programs in place to help me with certification?

    Yes! If you’re a veteran with G.I. Bill benefits, the Department of Veterans Affairs (VA) has a licensing and certification benefit that could help. This can help you obtain reimbursement for tests for a license or certification, such as some of those offered by AWS. These tests must be specifically approved for the G.I. Bill. Note: VA can pay only for the cost of the test and not other fees connected with obtaining a certification. You can learn more here, or check gibill.va.gov, where you’ll find a link to the Licensing and Certification page.
